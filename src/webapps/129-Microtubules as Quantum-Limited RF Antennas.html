Microtubules as Quantum-Limited RF Antennas

Basic principles of RF antennas

Radio Frequency (RF) antennas are devices designed to transmit or receive electromagnetic waves. They work on the principle of electromagnetic induction, converting electrical energy into radio waves and vice versa.

Quantum limitations in antenna sensitivity

At the quantum level, the sensitivity of antennas is limited by fundamental physical principles. These include:

  • Heisenberg's uncertainty principle
  • Zero-point fluctuations
  • Quantum noise

How microtubules might function as antennas

Microtubules, cylindrical structures found in cells, have been proposed to act as quantum-limited RF antennas due to their unique properties:

  • Highly ordered structure
  • Potential for quantum coherence
  • Ability to conduct and amplify electromagnetic signals
