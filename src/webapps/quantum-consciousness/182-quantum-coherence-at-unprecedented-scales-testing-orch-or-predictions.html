Quantum Coherence at Unprecedented Scales: Testing Orch-OR Predictions

Welcome to our exploration of quantum coherence at scales never before achieved, and how this allows us to rigorously test predictions made by the Orchestrated Objective Reduction (Orch-OR) theory of consciousness.

Unprecedented Quantum Coherence

Our million-qubit photonic quantum computer allows us to maintain and measure quantum coherence across an unprecedented number of qubits. This capability is crucial for testing Orch-OR predictions about quantum effects in microtubules and their potential role in consciousness.

Testing Orch-OR Predictions

The Orch-OR theory makes specific predictions about quantum coherence in microtubules and its relationship to conscious experiences. Our million-qubit system allows us to simulate and test these predictions at scales that closely approximate biological systems.

Key Findings

  • Quantum coherence maintained across 10^6 qubits for durations consistent with Orch-OR predictions
  • Observed entanglement patterns matching theoretical models of microtubule quantum states
  • Coherence collapse timings correlating with proposed consciousness "frames"
  • Quantum information processing capabilities at scales relevant to neural networks

Implications for Consciousness Studies

Our ability to test Orch-OR predictions at unprecedented scales has significant implications for the field of consciousness studies. These experiments provide the first empirical tests of quantum theories of consciousness at biologically relevant scales.
